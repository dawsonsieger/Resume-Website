Who I Am

I’m Dawson Sieger, an Accessibility Specialist passionate about creating inclusive digital experiences. With deep expertise in screen reader testing and WCAG audits, I help organizations build web and mobile content that truly works for everyone.

Driven by Access, Grounded in Impact

I believe accessibility is about more than compliance — it's about dignity, independence, and opportunity.

One experience that continues to shape my work involved a blind law student who was falling behind academically. His materials were mostly accessible, but he lacked the training to navigate them effectively. His university recognized the gap and reached out to me — not because they were required to, but because they believed in his potential.

I worked closely with the student through one-on-one training, helping him master the tools he needed to succeed. At the same time, I partnered with the university to identify and remediate inaccessible documents and assignments, ensuring better access for others down the line.

That experience reminded me of what really drives me: empowering individuals while helping institutions rise to the moment. Because accessibility isn’t just about checking boxes — it’s about creating the conditions for people to thrive.

Audits, Accessibility... and a Cat

(...plus another cat, and two very opinionated dogs.)

When I’m not deep in WCAG audits or training someone on screen reader navigation, I’m usually spending time with my fiancé and four pets — two dogs and two cats — who keep things lively (and occasionally chaotic).

I’m also an avid gamer. Whether I’m exploring immersive story-driven worlds or thinking about accessibility in game menus, I love how interactive media brings people together — when designed right.

I believe that accessibility work is ultimately about people. And having time for joy, curiosity, and connection off-screen is just as important as getting the code right.
